# Air Pollution Risk & Sustainability Impact Analysis
Optimising up to £50 million in environmental funding across 24,000+ English schools

[View the Interactive Dashboard on Tableau Public](https://public.tableau.com/app/profile/kris.noon/viz/Air_Quality_Analysis_Around_English_Schools/Dashboard1)

## Snapshot

- 24,049 schools analysed
- 185 pupils protected per £1,000 invested
- 3 to 4 times higher efficiency through exposure-based targeting
- £5M optimised allocation protects ~6,250 pupils
- Even distribution protects between 1,500 - 2,000 pupils
- Top 1,000 schools (based on deprivation) identified for highest marginal impact
- £3.2B estimated requirement to cover all schools

This project converts environmental exposure data into funding optimisation decisions by stakeholders.

## Sustainability Problem

Air pollution exposure disproportionately affects children in high-density urban areas.

### Key question:
How can limited environmental funding maximise measurable health protection per £ invested?

## Core KPIs

### Environmental Exposure
- PM2.5 concentration levels
- NO2 concentration levels
- Exposure bands: Very High, High, Moderate, Low
- Geographic clustering of pollution risk

### Impact Efficiency
- Pupils protected per £1,000
- Cost per pupil protected
- ROI by intervention type
- Budget efficiency multiplier

### Scenario Modelling
- Total pupils protected by budget size
- Regional ROI comparison
- Priority ranking of schools by marginal return

## Key Findings

### Intervention Efficiency

- Classroom air filtration: 185 pupils per £1,000
- Whole-school systems: ~10 times higher cost per pupil
- Green barriers: low measurable return relative to cost

High-impact, scalable interventions outperform capital-intensive upgrades.

### Geographic Concentration

- Pollution exposure is highly clustered
- London shows 85 percent higher ROI potential than the next authority (Birmingham)
- Uniform allocation significantly reduces impact

Targeted investment delivers 3 - 4 times more protection than equal distribution of the budget.

### Budget Impact Comparison

| Budget | Optimised Allocation   | Even Allocation         |
|--------|------------------------|-------------------------|
| £5M    | ~6,250 pupils          | 1,500 to 2,000 pupils   |
| £50M   | Compounding efficiency | Diluted marginal return |

Smaller budgets generate higher health impacts when exposure data drives budget assignment.

## Strategic Recommendations

1. Prioritise classroom-level air filtration in highest exposure clusters  
2. Allocate funding based on exposure intensity, not regional equality  
3. Begin with the top 1,000 highest marginal impact schools  
4. Phase infrastructure upgrades after high-ROI interventions are exhausted  

## Sustainability Impact

- Measurable reduction in child pollution exposure
- Transparent ROI framework for public sector sustainability decisions
- Data-driven prioritisation aligned with ESG objectives
